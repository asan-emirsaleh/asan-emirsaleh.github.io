## Overview

This dataset contains a curated collection list of short audio recordings illustrating non-standard phonological phenomena in Crimean Tatar dialects from the Sudak (Sudaq) region, Crimea. The recording were conducted by Asan Emirsale, Girey Bair, Refat Izzet, Enver Mecit, during 2009-2026. The recordings deposited on video hosting platforms were also incorporated. The recordings are annotated with IPA transcription.

### Glottalization
Ejective consonants, also being referred to as explosives, are characterized by a sharp glottal clousure that is absent in standard Crimean Tatar as well in most Turkic languages. The full range of phonemes documented includes both palatalized and regular correlates.

#### Palatalized ejective consonants
Several palatalized ejectives are attested in the local language of Sudaq region: **tʲ'**, **qʲ'**, **kxʲ'**, **kʲ'**, **gʲ'**.

## Source of data
The dataset is constructed using both original field recordings made by compilers and publicly available recordings such as those deposited on video hosting platforms or radio archives.

Field recordings were conducted between 2008 and 2025 all across Crimea where repatriated people of Sudaq origin are settled. The speakers represent  from Büyük Taraqtaş and Küçük Taraqtaş, Xutlax, Qoz and Xapsxor of the oghuz-type language spoken in the Sudaq region, covering both eastern and western branches of the Oghuz-type dialect of the Sudaq region.

Publicly available recordings were sourced from YouTube, including material published by Qaradeñiz Production and "Qırım Akademiyası" initiative.
